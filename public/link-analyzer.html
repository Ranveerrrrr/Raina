🌐 Port Scanning Awareness

What is Port Scanning? Port scanning is the process of sending packets to specific ports on a host and analyzing the responses to learn about the state of those ports.

  • Open Port: Accepting connections (may reveal a running service).
  • Closed Port: No service listening, but host is reachable.
  • Filtered Port: No response or blocked by firewall.

🔍 Tools like nmap are commonly used for port scanning in penetration testing or network audits.

⚠️ Unauthorized port scanning is considered illegal or unethical in many environments. Only perform it on systems you own or have permission to scan.
